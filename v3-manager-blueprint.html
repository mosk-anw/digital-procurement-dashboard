Technical Architecture & Stack

Building a robust "Digital Procurement Manager" requires a tiered architecture. We separate the **Experience Layer** (Apps) from the **Intelligence Layer** (AI) and the **Transactional Layer** (Core Logic).

Note on "Medusa": Medusa.js is excellent as the headless commerce engine (handling products, carts, orders). However, for the complex "Many-to-Many" logic of procurement (1 Restaurant -> 10 Suppliers, splitting 1 PO into 5), we need a custom logic layer on top, or heavy customization of Medusa's plugins.

The AI Engine: SKU Normalization

One of the biggest challenges in F&B is messy data. Supplier A sells "Tomatoes 5kg Box", Supplier B sells "Tomato Loose kg". The AI Engine uses fuzzy matching and semantic understanding to map these to a single Master SKU: "Fresh Tomato".

  • Ingests unstructured Order/Invoice Lists.
  • Maps Unit of Measure (UOM) automatically.
  • Enables true Price Comparison across suppliers.
